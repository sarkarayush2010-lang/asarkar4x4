Features:
Translucent case(or not if you choose to print in a non-translucent filament, but why not transparent tech???)
128x32 OLED Display
16 (yes SIXTEEN) fully customizable keys(thats like enough to play minecraft)
and ITS MY FIRST PCB YAY!!!

Bill of Materials:
16x Cherry MX Switches
16x DSA Keycaps
4x M3x5x4 Heatset inserts
4x M3x16mm SHCS Bolts
PCB
1x 0.91" 128x32 OLED Display
20x 1N4148 DO-35 Diodes.
1x XIAO RP2040
1x Case(2x 3d-printed parts)

That should be all!
Theres still an extra gpio pin, so maybe u could hardwire something else like an led or something cool in the future!

3d Printed parts
16x diodes
Solder
optional(something to cover the screen at the top of the model)

CAD Model:
Everything fits together using 5 M3 Bolts and heatset inserts. You can just use a soldering iron for those!
Prints in two peices, and the parts use a SANDWICH MOUNT to hold the PCB in place. 
Oled and microcontroller have a little display window. It is not filled in with anything, but you can use those clear boxes that you get when you buy strawberries and cut those and hot glue it to the top of the case to look cool! 
Made in onshape

PCB:
Made in Ki-CAD
It's my first PCB! It uses 20 diodes

Firmware
Uses Basic QMK Firmware for everything.
Firmware given is just for the keypad. Oled is for you(and me) to make later! Or just leave the oled blank lol.
